The difference between a million and a billion is astronomical. A billion is 1,000 times larger than a million.

The Billionaire Reality

If someone earned $5,000 per day (more than most people make in a month), every single day since Columbus reached the Americas in 1492, they still wouldn't have $1 billion yet.

They'd need to keep earning until the year 2040 to reach $1 billion.

Why This Matters

The human brain struggles to comprehend the scale of a billion. We use time as a reference because it's something we all experience equally. Whether you're a billionaire or work minimum wage, we all get the same 86,400 seconds per day.

This visualization aims to make the incomprehensible comprehensible‚Äîto show that the difference between a million and a billion isn't just three zeros, it's the difference between 11 days and 32 years.
