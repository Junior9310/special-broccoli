ğŸ›¡ï¸ How Does the Fed Stay Independent?

The Founding Fathers (and later lawmakers) created some clever rules:

14-Year Terms

Fed governors serve for 14 years! That's longer than any president (max 8 years). This means they don't have to worry about pleasing whoever's in the White House.

Can't Be Fired Easily

The President can't fire Fed officials just because they disagree. They can only be removed "for cause" (meaning they did something really wrong).

No Government Funding Needed

The Fed makes its own money from interest on loans, so Congress can't threaten to cut its budget. It's financially independent too!

Experts, Not Politicians

Fed officials are economists and banking experts, not people running for office. They're chosen for their brains, not their popularity! ğŸ§
