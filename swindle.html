üìú The Emoluments Clause

The U.S. Constitution's Foreign Emoluments Clause (Article I, Section 9) prohibits federal officials from receiving payments from foreign governments without Congressional consent. The Domestic Emoluments Clause prohibits the President from receiving any emolument from federal or state governments beyond their salary.

Constitutional Text:

"...no Person holding any Office of Profit or Trust under them, shall, without the Consent of the Congress, accept of any present, Emolument, Office, or Title, of any kind whatever, from any King, Prince, or foreign State."

The Issue: Trump refused to divest from his businesses or place them in a blind trust, instead transferring day-to-day operations to his sons while maintaining ownership. This meant foreign and domestic governments could‚Äîand did‚Äîspend money at his properties while he was president.
